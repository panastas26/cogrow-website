The Speed-to-Lead Guide: Why the First 5 Minutes Decide If You Win the Job

CoGrow Team July 8, 2026 5 min read

Here's something most service business owners underestimate: by the time you get around to calling a new lead back, they've often already moved on.

A lead that comes in while your team is on jobs can sit for hours, sometimes the better part of a day, before anyone responds. By then your prospect has likely called a couple of competitors, booked one of them, and forgotten your name.

What the Data Suggests About Speed-to-Lead

Industry research on lead response has consistently pointed to the same thing: the company that responds first tends to win the business. Not the cheapest, and not the one with the best reviews. The first one to actually have a conversation.

The same research suggests that responding within a few minutes makes you far more likely to reach and qualify a lead than waiting half an hour or more. The odds drop sharply the longer you wait.

For owner-operated service businesses — plumbers, HVAC techs, electricians, CPAs, law firms — this is a metric most owners don't track. You're spending real money on Google Ads, SEO, and referral networks. If you can't respond within a few minutes, you're getting less out of that budget than you should.

The After-Hours Problem

It gets harder after hours. A lot of leads come in during evenings, weekends, and holidays. These are high-intent prospects: a clogged drain, a tax deadline, a legal question that can't wait until Monday.

What happens to those leads? They hit voicemail. And voicemail is where leads go to die. Most callers won't leave a message at all — they hang up and call the next company on the list.

Picture How This Plays Out

Take a typical plumbing shop: good reviews, steady ad spend, experienced techs, but the office is slammed and a lot of leads only get a callback hours later, if at all. The leads are already paid for. The drop-off happens entirely in the gap between the call coming in and someone responding.

Close that gap — answer or text back within seconds, day or night — and more of those same leads turn into booked jobs. Nothing about the marketing changed. The only difference is that nobody got left waiting.

How to Close Your Speed-to-Lead Gap

You don't need to hire a 24/7 call center. You need a system that does three things:

  1. Responds instantly — within seconds, not minutes. A text, a chat message, or an AI voice response that acknowledges the lead and starts the conversation.
  2. Qualifies the lead — asks the right questions (service needed, location, urgency) so you're not wasting time on bad fits.
  3. Books the appointment — gets the prospect on your calendar before they have time to call your competitor.

An automated lead-response system handles that first touchpoint for you. Your team still does the work and closes the job; the system just makes sure no lead sits waiting, day or night.

Where This Leaves You

Speed-to-lead isn't a vanity metric. For most service businesses it's one of the biggest levers they aren't pulling. Respond in a few minutes and you're in the running for the job. Wait a few hours and you've usually lost it before the conversation ever started.
