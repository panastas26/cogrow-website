The 5-Touch Follow-Up Sequence That Converts Cold Leads Into Booked Appointments

CoGrow Team December 9, 2024 4 min read

Here's the uncomfortable truth about follow-up: 48% of salespeople never follow up with a lead even once. And of those who do, most quit after a single attempt.

Meanwhile, research consistently shows that 80% of sales require at least 5 follow-up touches after the initial contact. The gap between "one and done" and "five touches" is where most service businesses are leaving tens of thousands of dollars on the table every year.

If you run a plumbing company, HVAC business, law firm, CPA practice, or any owner-operated service business, this guide gives you the exact follow-up sequence to turn cold leads into booked appointments.

Why Most Businesses Only Follow Up Once

It's not laziness. It's logistics. When you're the one doing the work, managing the team, and running the business, following up with leads who didn't book on the first call falls to the bottom of the list. Every day that passes makes it feel more awkward to reach back out.

The result? A massive pile of "warm-ish" leads that slowly go cold. These are people who expressed real interest in your service but didn't convert on the first touch — and nobody ever reached out again.

The 5-Touch Follow-Up Sequence

This sequence is designed for service businesses. The timing, channels, and messaging are based on what actually works across hundreds of campaigns we've analyzed.

1

Instant Response

Within 1 minute • SMS

A text message acknowledging their inquiry and asking one qualifying question. Keep it short, personal, and end with a question to start a conversation.

"Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out to [Business]. I'd love to help — can you tell me a bit about what you're looking for so I can point you to the right person?"
2

Value-Add Follow-Up

24 hours later • Email

Don't just say "checking in." Send something useful — a relevant tip, a link to a helpful resource, or a quick piece of advice related to their inquiry. Position yourself as the expert, not a pest.

"Hi [Name], I put together a quick checklist of [relevant topic] that might help while you're evaluating options. [Link] — Happy to walk through any of this if you'd like to chat."
3

Direct Ask

Day 3 • SMS

A short, direct text with a specific time offer. Make it easy to say yes by removing the decision friction.

"Hey [Name], I have a few openings this week if you'd like to get this taken care of. Would [Day] morning or afternoon work better?"
4

Social Proof

Day 7 • Email

Share a testimonial, case study, or before/after from a similar client. Let someone else do the selling for you.

"Hi [Name], wanted to share a quick story — we helped [similar client type] with [similar problem] last month and [specific result]. If you're still looking for help with this, I'd love to do the same for you."
5

Break-Up Message

Day 14 • SMS

The "last chance" message. Let them know you're closing out the inquiry. This creates urgency without pressure — and surprisingly, it gets the highest response rate of any touch in the sequence.

"Hi [Name], I don't want to keep bugging you so this will be my last message. If you ever need help with [service], we're here. Just reply to this text anytime."

Why This Sequence Works

Each touch serves a different purpose:

  • Touch 1 captures attention while intent is highest.
  • Touch 2 builds credibility and keeps you top of mind.
  • Touch 3 makes it easy to convert with a specific offer.
  • Touch 4 uses social proof to overcome remaining objections.
  • Touch 5 leverages scarcity and creates a natural decision point.

The mix of SMS and email matters too. Texts get a 98% open rate compared to ~20% for email. But email lets you send longer, richer content. Using both channels maximizes your visibility without feeling spammy.

Automate It or It Won't Happen

Here's the hard truth: if this sequence depends on you or your team remembering to send each message manually, it won't happen. You'll do it for a week, get busy, and it'll fall apart.

The businesses that actually see results from follow-up are the ones that automate the entire sequence. When a new lead comes in, the system triggers automatically. Every touch fires at the right time, through the right channel, with the right message — no manual effort required.

And when a lead replies at any point in the sequence? The system pauses the automation and alerts your team so they can take over the conversation personally.
